Open Science Summer School 2025

The LMU Open Science Center (OSC) is excited to announce the Open Science Summer School 2025 (OSSS25). This dual-track intensive 5-day Open Science Summer School aims to provide participants with two sets of skills:

Track 1: Open Science knowledge and skills

In this track, you will learn to make your research more transparent, reproducible and credible in the eyes of your peers, the public, and funding agencies. This track consists of public lectures accessible to anyone as well as hands-on practical workshops for a selected number of applicants. Selected applicants are expected to attend all sessions from Monday 15.09.2025 9:00 to Friday 19.09.2025 14:30.

By joining this track, you will learn how to:
- Specify your research design and set up your statistical plans in advance of collecting data to prevent biases in analyses, with the help of preregistration and data simulation;
- Create computationally reproducible workflows so you can be more efficient and spot mistakes in data wrangling or analyses, through programming with version controlled scripts;
- Prepare and share data and code in connection with your articles by applying the Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable principles, and using adequate repositories and licenses to accumulate credit and citations.

Target audience
Early career researchers who are Open Science novices (e.g., advanced Masters students, PhD students, postdocs).

Selection criteria
Clear motivations and plan for implementation of Open Science skills. At equal scores, LMU members or upcoming LMU members will be favored over external students and researchers.

Format
Hybrid

Track 2: Open Science instructor training

In this track, you will develop essential pedagogical skills to promote Open Science in your teaching and research environments - no prior experience in teaching required! Selected applicants are expected to attend all sessions from Friday 19.09.2025 14:30 to 17:30.

By joining this track, you will learn how to:
- Design and deliver Open Science-focused workshops, and apply basic didactic techniques to diverse audiences;
- Apply activation methods in the classroom to foster motivation and attention;
- Develop skills to advocate for Open Science, to lead research groups and discussions around Open Science and to analyse the potential challenges and issues around the implementation of Open Science practises.

After joining this track, you will have the opportunity to put your new skills into practice and co-deliver a workshop at LMU (e.g. in your department) on an Open Science topic of your choice within six months after the workshop. If you choose to do so, the OSC will support you during the planning, preparation and delivery of the workshop with hands-on feedback and practical tips!

Target audience
Early career researchers or research support staff interested in becoming Open Science instructors at student/researcher-level.

Eligibility criteria
- LMU member
- Proof of acquired Open Science knowledge and skills (e.g. application for track 1, certificate of attendance to previous Open Science training, link to open research resources such as a GitHub repository, FAIR dataset, or open preregistration).

Selection criteria
Clear motivations. At equal scores, those with a concrete plan for delivery will be favored.

Format
Hybrid
